Release Angle at Ground Level

Q: A projectile is released from ground level and lands at ground level. At what angle $\theta$ above the horizontal should the projectile be released to maximize range?

Let $s$ denote the initial speed of the projectile at the moment of release. From physics, the kinematic equations are

\[ x(t)=ut \]
\[ y(t)=-0.5gt^2+vt=-16t^2+vt, \]

where $g=32\text{ ft/s}^2$ is the magnitude of acceleration due to gravity, and $u=s\cos\theta$ and $v=s\sin\theta$ are the horizontal and vertical components of initial velocity.

Setting $y(t)=0$ and dividing by $t$, we can solve for the nonzero solution:

\[ t_{\text{air}}=\frac{v}{16}. \]

Time in air is directly proportional to $v$.

The range is the product of the horizontal speed and the time in air. Let $r$ denote range.

\[ r=u(t_{\text{air}})=\frac{uv}{16}. \]

Since $r$ is directly proportional to the symmetric product $uv$ and the constraint equation $u^2+v^2=s^2$ is symmetric in $u$ and $v$, the maximum value of $r$ occurs when $u=v$. If you used the optimization method from Section 4.5 in your Calculus book to find the maximum, the roles of $u$ and $v$ would be interchangeable.

Therefore, the optimal release angle is $45^\circ$.
